## Books

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="iOS App Reverse Engineering" icon="book">
    **By Snakeninny et al.**
    
    The definitive guide to iOS reverse engineering, covering everything from basic concepts to advanced techniques. Includes practical examples and real-world case studies.
    
    *Topics: Runtime analysis, class-dump, Cycript, Theos, Frida*
  </Card>

  <Card title="Hacking and Securing iOS Applications" icon="book">
    **By Jonathan Zdziarski**
    
    A comprehensive guide to iOS security from the perspective of both attackers and defenders. Essential reading for understanding iOS security architecture.
    
    *Topics: Encryption, keychain, sandboxing, code signing*
  </Card>

  <Card title="iOS Hacker's Handbook" icon="book">
    **By Charlie Miller et al.**
    
    Deep dive into iOS vulnerabilities and exploitation techniques. While some content is dated, the fundamental concepts remain valuable.
    
    *Topics: ARM assembly, kernel exploitation, fuzzing*
  </Card>

  <Card title="Learning iOS Security" icon="book">
    **By Allister Banks & Charles Edge**
    
    Practical guide to iOS security mechanisms and how to work with them during reverse engineering and security research.
    
    *Topics: Touch ID, App Transport Security, secure coding*
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Legal and Ethical Resources

<Warning>
  Understanding the legal and ethical implications of reverse engineering is crucial.
</Warning>

### Important Considerations

- **Copyright Law** - Understand DMCA Section 1201 and fair use exceptions
- **Terms of Service** - Many apps prohibit reverse engineering in their TOS
- **Responsible Disclosure** - Learn proper vulnerability disclosure procedures
- **Research Ethics** - Follow ethical guidelines for security research
